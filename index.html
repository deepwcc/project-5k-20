Key Principles
🇳🇴
Norwegian Sub-Threshold
Core of the plan. 1000m reps at P4:40–4:55 with 60s rest. HR should sit just below LT2. If HR climbs above 173, slow down — aerobic adaptation is the goal.
📉
HR as Ground Truth
Pace targets are estimates. Trust your HR zones over pace. Fitness improvement shows as lower HR at the same pace — not faster times in training.
🔁
9-Day Cycle Logic
Two quality sessions per cycle, separated by easy days. This spacing allows real recovery between hard efforts and prevents the cumulative fatigue that derails 7-day plans.
🦶
Injury Protocol
If Achilles or plantar fascia flares: drop to easy Z2 only, no speed work until 48h symptom-free. Proximal Achilles is lower-risk but still demands respect.
⚡
Time Trial (D18)
Not just a test — it's the calibration event that sets all formal pace zones. Go out controlled (km 1 at 4:28–4:32), then let HR guide the middle km, empty the tank in km 5.
💪
Strength Training
2× per week. Lower body (squat, RDL, farmer carry) away from hard run days. Upper body can pair with easy runs. Goal is injury prevention, not muscle gain.
